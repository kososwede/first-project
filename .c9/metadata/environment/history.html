Strängnäs was the native city of prominent reformer Laurentius Andreae and the home city of both Andreae and Olaus Petri. It became a regional centre of education and scholarship, and in 1626 the Thomas Gymnasium was established by King Gustavus"," Adolphus, and is today Sweden's second oldest operating gymnasium. The urban and economic development of Strängnäs seems to have slowed after the Reformation, only flourishing temporarily with the arrival of energetic bishops. The city was slow"," to engage with the 19th century industrial development and investment found in other areas. A significant fire in 1871 led to large-scale reconstruction of the city, from which time its current appearance stems.
